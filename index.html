About Me

Hello! I am a third-year PhD student in Computer and Information Science at the University of Pennsylvania, advised by Prof. Chris-Callison Burch. My research interests are in natural language processing (NLP). My current focus is on cross-lingual and multi-lingual applications of NLP, especially as they pertain to improving the generalization and low-resource capabilities of large-scale language models. I am also involved in efforts in summarization and commonsense inference.

I received BA and MS degrees in Computer Science from Columbia University, where I also minored in Linguistics and Economics. At Columbia I was advised by Julia Hirschberg and Kathleen McKeown. Prior to beginning my PhD, I worked at Amazon in the AWS AI and Alexa NLU teams.

In addition to research, I am also dedicated towards teaching, and have served as a teaching assistant for computer science courses, introductory to graduate-level.
