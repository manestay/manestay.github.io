About Me

Hello! I am a final-year PhD student in Computer and Information Science at the University of Pennsylvania, advised by Prof. Chris-Callison Burch. My research focuses on the multilingual capabilities of LLMs, with two overarching themes. On natural language processing (NLP), I study what multilingual knowledge is encoded into LLMs, building benchmarks and efficient techniques. On computational social science (CSS), I study how LLMâ€™s knowledge can be used to understand contemporary society, with a particular focus on cross-cultural and cross-lingual concerns.

I received BA and MS degrees in Computer Science from Columbia University, where I also minored in Linguistics and Economics. At Columbia I was advised by Profs. Julia Hirschberg and Kathleen McKeown. I have also pursued several research internships in industry (Microsoft, Amazon, Google). I am active in the broader CS community through mentorship, teaching assistant roles, and conference reviewing.

As of January 2025, I am on the industry job market for research scientist positions. To provide a quick overview, I bring with me experience in both academic and industry settings, as well as insight into all aspects of LLM development, from training such systems to investigating their practical applications.
