About Me

Hello! I am a final-year PhD student in Computer and Information Science at the University of Pennsylvania, advised by Prof. Chris-Callison Burch. My research interests are in the cross-lingual applications of NLP. I am currently focused on assessing the multilingual capabilities of LLMs through novel tasks and evaluation, and in designing training-time and inference-time techniques to efficiently improve these capabilities. In the past (and present), I have broadly collaborated on projects in computational social science, machine translation, and summarization.

I received BA and MS degrees in Computer Science from Columbia University, where I also minored in Linguistics and Economics. At Columbia I was advised by Profs. Julia Hirschberg and Kathleen McKeown. I have also pursued several research internships in industry (Microsoft, Amazon, Google).

I am active in the CS community through mentorship, teaching assistant roles, and conference reviewing. Feel free to get in touch!
